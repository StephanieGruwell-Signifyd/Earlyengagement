Merchant Onboarding

Why Early Intervention Wins

Three risks that quietly derail Go Live — act on yellow flags before they turn red.

Leadership & Champion Changes
Stakeholder Risk
Layoffs & Restructuring
Our champion(s) are no longer be at the company.
Shifting Economic Priorities
Economic factors drive new priorities — Signifyd can slip behind budget scrutiny and internal pivots.
New Leadership, Lost Buy-In
Promotions or external hires bring in stakeholders with new point of view which can derail the project.
▼ Signals & actions
Impact
Champion is gone. Without someone who owns the outcome, urgency erodes and Go Live becomes aspirational rather than committed.
Yellow Flag Signals
New name appears on invites and/or requests to revisit the original ROI or business case.
Our Move
Re-sell the value. Treat every new stakeholder as a new champion to earn — walk them through the business case, the contract context, and what's at stake for their business.
When to Act
At the first sign of a roster change — before the new stakeholder builds other commitments.
Capacity, Context & Competing Demands
Technical Resource Risk
No Connection to the Business Case
Technical teams assigned post-sale weren't in the room when priorities were set — they're executing a ticket, not championing an outcome.
Competing Priorities & Code Freezes
Engineering bandwidth shifts to other projects, and predictable freezes like end-of-year become silent timeline killers when not planned around.
Data & Domain Knowledge Gaps
If the right SMEs aren't engaged early, gaps in source data understanding surface mid-integration and cause costly delays.
▼ Signals & actions
Impact
Implementation stalls without drama — tasks slip, timelines extend, and no single person is accountable for re-starting momentum.
Yellow Flag Signals
Tasks going past due with no update. Vague answers on timelines. Engineers missing calls or deprioritizing Signifyd tickets.
Our Move
Ensure stakeholders actively communicate priority to their technical teams. Surface freeze windows early and plan around them. Identify data SMEs before integration begins.
When to Act
During kickoff — not after the first missed milestone.
Go Live Commitment & Follow-Through
Timeline Risk
Soft Go Live Commitment
Pre-sales dates are often aspirational. Without a formal re-commitment post-contract, the target becomes a suggestion, not a deadline.
Repeated Date Slippage
Each pushed date erodes urgency. A pattern of delays signals a prioritization problem — not just a scheduling one.
Missed Tasks & Slow Engagement
When merchants fall behind on their own milestones — configs, test data, looping in the right people — the timeline compresses on their side, not ours.
▼ Signals & actions
Impact
A delayed Go Live is lost value for the merchant and lost revenue for Signifyd. The longer it takes, the harder it becomes to re-establish urgency.
Yellow Flag Signals
Go Live date moves more than once. Merchant tasks routinely missed or handed off. No urgency when rescheduling. Calls hard to get on the calendar.
Our Move
Establish a formal Go Live commitment with joint accountability. Review task ownership regularly. Escalate delays early — a slipping task is an early warning, not just an inconvenience.
When to Act
At contract signature — then reinforced at every milestone checkpoint.

"Deviaton Zero" Principle — The goal is to act so fast on a change that the "deviation" from the goal remains at zero. Communicate early and often. We all need to be ready to act . Immediate visibility turns 'surprises' into 'strategy.' It allows for proactive resource shifts that keep us at Deviation Zero and protect our quarterly commitments.
